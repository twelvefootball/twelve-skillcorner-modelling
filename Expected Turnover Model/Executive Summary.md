# Executive Summary

### The Context and Attribution Problem
Traditional defensive football metrics evaluate isolated on-ball events, such as tackles, interceptions, and defensive duels. However, these individualistic statistics fail to capture pressing as a synchronised, spatiotemporal team behaviour. Furthermore, evaluating collective defensive actions introduces a significant analytical challenge: attributing fair individual credit within a multi-player pressing chain. This issue is colloquially termed as the "Van Dijk Effect"—a phenomenon where the structural brilliance of an elite defender inflates the apparent performance of surrounding teammates. From a recruitment perspective, failing to separate individual skill from team structure risks the acquisition of players reliant on systemic support rather than intrinsic ability. Consequently, football clubs require a rigorous mathematical framework to isolate the true, independent value a player adds to a defensive sequence.

### Methodology and Predictive Modelling
To resolve this attribution problem, this thesis constructs a machine learning architecture utilising continuous tracking and dynamic event data provided by SkillCorner. The core of the pipeline is an XGBoost classifier, trained to predict sequence-level Expected Turnover (xTO), thereby quantifying the probability that a given defensive pressing chain will successfully disrupt possession. Crucially, the research implements an exact Game-Theoretic Shapley value framework to solve the individual attribution dilemma. By computationally evaluating every player's presence and absence across all possible combinatorial subsets of an event chain, the framework isolates the true, fractional marginal xTO of each participant. The pipeline dynamically enforces a Total Value Recovery step, guaranteeing that the aggregate of all individual marginal contributions strictly equals the holistic xTO generated by the chain itself, rendering the metric both causally interpretable and fully additive over a season.

### Derived Metrics and Tactical Findings
Alongside the primary marginal xTO metric, the pipeline derives a comprehensive suite of contextual sub-metrics to accurately map a player's complete Out-of-Possession (OOP) profile. These metrics include pressing Volume (Chains per 90), Efficiency (xTO per Chain), Dominance (Contribution Share), and Self-Sufficiency (Solo Pressing Ratio). Moreover, the research establishes novel structural parameters: "Shape Discipline" and the "Exposure Index" (quantifying the pressing risk ratio as Expected Threat conceded per 1.0 xTO generated). Analysis of these indicators demonstrates significant efficacy in differentiating structurally disciplined, highly intelligent pressing players from reckless defenders who jeopardise their team's defensive shape despite recording traditionally high defensive volumes.

### Practical Application and Deployment
To bridge the gap between predictive modelling and elite football operations, this pipeline is operationalised into an interactive web application using Streamlit. Serving as a dedicated tactical dashboard, it translates complex mathematical outputs into actionable insights. Featuring a "Scouting Room" for the comparative analysis of player percentiles by position, and a "Film Room" that reconstructs historical pressing chains with animated, Shapley-attributed visualisations mapped onto pitch geometry, the deployment delivers concrete analytical value. Ultimately, this tool provides scouting and coaching departments with a data-driven platform for recruitment, opponent analysis, and tactical feedback.
